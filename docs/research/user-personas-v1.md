**Purpose:** Define the primary user segments based on goals, reward knowledge, financial behavior, and travel aspirations. These personas guide product decisions, UX design, recommendation logic, and feature prioritization.

---

# Framework

Personas are behavioral models (not demographic profiles), defined across four dimensions: travel goals, reward knowledge, spending capacity, optimization intent.

---

# Persona 1 — The Travel Aspirant *(primary MVP persona)*

Dreams of premium travel but doesn't know how to use rewards effectively.

- **Profile:** 22–35, salaried professional, ₹30,000–₹80,000/month spend, 1–2 international trips/year, cashback or beginner travel cards.
- **Goals:** first business-class flight; luxury hotel stays on points; maximize travel value without dramatically increasing spending.
- **Pain points:** doesn't know which cards to choose; reward ecosystems confusing; unsure how many points are required; distrusts conflicting blog advice.
- **Current behavior:** YouTube, Reddit, blogs, "Best credit cards in India" searches; uses cashback cards with no travel strategy.
- **Success:** "I now have a clear, realistic plan to achieve my travel goal."
- **Product opportunities:** goal-based onboarding, education through explanations, simple execution plans, clear progress tracking.

# Persona 2 — The Reward Optimizer *(secondary)*

Already understands travel rewards; wants to maximize value.

- **Profile:** 28–45, ₹75,000+/month, multiple premium cards, travels frequently.
- **Goals:** optimize spend allocation, maximize transfer value, reduce opportunity cost, compare strategies.
- **Pain points:** complex rules, frequent program changes, manual calculations, spreadsheet fatigue.
- **Success:** "OptiMiles gives me a better strategy than I could build manually."
- **Product opportunities:** advanced simulations, strategy comparison, spend routing, transfer intelligence.

# Persona 3 — The Cashback Converter *(secondary)*

Uses cashback cards; wants to transition to travel rewards.

- **Profile:** ₹25,000–₹60,000/month; SBI Cashback / Amazon Pay ICICI / Flipkart Axis; minimal airline/hotel reward knowledge.
- **Goals:** understand whether travel rewards are worth pursuing; decide if a premium card is justified; earn travel benefits without overspending.
- **Pain points:** cashback-vs-miles uncertainty; annual-fee hesitancy; transfer partners opaque.
- **Success:** "I understand why switching to travel rewards helps me achieve my goal."
- **Product opportunities:** cashback-vs-travel comparisons, ROI explanations, beginner-friendly guidance.

# Persona 4 — The Premium Traveler *(future)*

Owns premium cards; wants maximum returns.

- **Profile:** ₹1,00,000+/month, premium cardholder, frequent international traveler.
- **Goals:** extract maximum value from premium cards; optimize transfer timing; avoid devaluations.
- **Pain points:** tracking changing programs; identifying best redemptions; managing multiple ecosystems.
- **Success:** "I consistently receive the highest value from my existing cards."
- **Product opportunities:** transfer intelligence, advanced optimization, reward news/alerts (future roadmap).

---

# Prioritization

- **Primary (MVP):** The Travel Aspirant — drives landing-page messaging, onboarding, recommendation engine, dashboard design, AI explanations.
- **Secondary:** Reward Optimizer, Cashback Converter — supported, not the design target.
- **Future:** Premium Traveler — expands with advanced optimization, monitoring, multi-goal planning.

# Design implications

| Product area | Decision |
|---|---|
| Landing page | Lead with travel goals, not credit cards |
| Onboarding | Conversational goal discovery |
| Recommendation engine | Explain reasoning, not just rankings |
| Dashboard | Highlight goal achievability first |
| AI layer | Educate while recommending |
| Roadmap | Advanced optimization only after core goal planning is validated |

# MVP persona focus

> A travel aspirant with moderate monthly spending who wants to achieve a premium travel goal but lacks the knowledge to build an effective reward strategy.

If this persona can confidently answer *"Can I achieve my travel goal, and what should I do next?"*, the core value proposition is delivered.

**Future persona expansion (excluded from MVP):** Business Expense Optimizer, Family Travel Planner, Digital Nomad, Luxury Hotel Enthusiast, Frequent Flyer Elite, Small Business Owner.
