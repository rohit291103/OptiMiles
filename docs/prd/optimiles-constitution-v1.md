# Purpose

This document defines what OptiMiles is, what it is not, and the principles that guide every product, engineering, data, and AI decision.

When faced with competing priorities, this document takes precedence over feature requests, roadmap ideas, and implementation preferences.

---

# Mission

> Turn everyday spending into extraordinary travel experiences through explainable reward optimization.

# Vision

> Become the Google Maps for travel rewards.

Just as Google Maps helps users find the best route to a destination, OptiMiles helps users find the best reward strategy to achieve a travel goal.

---

# Product Identity

OptiMiles is a goal-based travel reward optimization platform. The platform helps users:

- Achieve travel goals through reward optimization
- Maximize the value of their existing card portfolio
- Understand reward ecosystems and transfer partners
- Simulate reward accumulation journeys
- Make informed reward decisions through transparent reasoning

OptiMiles is not a card recommendation platform. Card recommendations are an output of optimization, not the product itself.

---

# Core Belief

> Most people either choose the wrong cards for their travel goals or fail to maximize the cards they already own.

OptiMiles exists to solve both problems. Before recommending new cards, OptiMiles should help users unlock more value from their existing cards, spending patterns, and reward ecosystems.

---

# User Journey Philosophy

Traditional reward products start with cards. OptiMiles starts with goals.

- Traditional flow: `Cards → Points → Redemption`
- OptiMiles flow: `Travel Goal → Required Rewards → Strategy → Card Portfolio → Spend Allocation → Transfer Plan → Redemption`

Goals drive strategy. Strategy drives card decisions. Not the other way around.

---

# Constitutional Principles

## 1. Goal First
Every recommendation must begin with a user objective (Singapore Airlines Business Class, Marriott luxury stay, airport lounge access, family vacation). The goal determines the strategy.

## 2. Maximize Before Recommending
Before recommending a new card: understand existing cards, identify unused benefits, identify optimization opportunities, improve spend allocation, improve redemption strategy. Only then recommend additional cards.

## 3. Optimization Over Comparison
OptiMiles does not answer *"What is the best card?"* It answers *"What is the best strategy for your goal?"* Comparison is a feature. Optimization is the product.

## 4. Explain Everything
Every recommendation should answer: Why this strategy? Why this card? Why this transfer partner? Why this spend allocation? What assumptions were used? What tradeoffs exist? Trust comes from transparency.

## 5. Rules Before AI
Reward calculations must come from structured reward intelligence.

AI **is** responsible for: intent extraction, reasoning, explanation, summarization.
AI is **not** responsible for: reward calculations, transfer ratios, reward valuations, optimization logic.

The system calculates. The AI explains.

## 6. Trust Above Growth
A smaller correct recommendation is better than a larger uncertain recommendation. When uncertain: show assumptions, show confidence levels, acknowledge unknowns. Never fabricate reward intelligence.

## 7. Reward Intelligence Over Reward Content
OptiMiles exists to help users make decisions, not to generate content. Prioritize optimization, simulations, recommendations, and planning over blogs, news feeds, card reviews, and generic educational content.

# Constitution Test

A feature should only be built if it improves at least one of the following:

1. **Reward Maximization** — does it help users extract more value from existing spending?
2. **Portfolio Optimization** — does it help users maximize existing cards and reward ecosystems?
3. **Optimization Accuracy** — does it improve recommendation quality or optimization accuracy?
4. **Simulation Quality** — does it improve reward projections and planning confidence?
5. **Explainability** — does it improve user understanding and trust?

If the answer to all five questions is no: the feature does not belong in the OptiMiles MVP.

# MVP Boundaries

The MVP focuses on travel rewards. Initial ecosystems:

- **Airlines:** Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, Air India Maharaja Club
- **Hotels:** Marriott Bonvoy, Accor Live Limitless

The MVP intentionally prioritizes depth over breadth. (Full scope: [mvp-scope-v2.md](mvp-scope-v2.md).)

# Success Definition

OptiMiles succeeds when a user can clearly understand:

- Where they want to go
- How many rewards they need
- Which strategy is optimal
- How long it will take
- Which actions they should take next

without needing to become a reward expert themselves.

# Founding Statement

> OptiMiles does not optimize for points earned. It optimizes for goals achieved.
