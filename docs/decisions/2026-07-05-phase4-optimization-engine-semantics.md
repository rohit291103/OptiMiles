# Decision Log ‚Äî Phase 4 Optimization Engine (feasibility, generation, ranking) + `acquirable` card flag

**Date:** 2026-07-05
**Area:** backend (Optimization Engine ‚Äî build-plan Phase 4)

## Context

Build-plan Phase 4 is the last of the three pure math engines: `optimization/` owns Stages 6 (feasibility gate), 7 (candidate generation) and 9 (ranking), scoring the Stage 8 simulated outcomes. Two requirements were explicitly deferred into this phase (catalog-expansion decision log + tracker): the `claimed_total_miles` reconciliation (generator claim vs simulated truth) and an `acquirable` card notion so the one-new-card archetype never recommends the discontinued Axis Atlas. TDD mandatory; `backend-reviewer` pass at the end. The Product_intelligence conflict rule still stands (implemented/planned beats the doc). Engines stay pure (no DB, no LLM; only Valuation's pure math may be imported).

## Decisions

1. **A fourth internal module, `optimization/allocation.py`, carries the shared allocation + accumulation math** (not a sixth engine ‚Äî it's a private helper of Stages 6‚Äì7, same pattern as `valuation/transfer_math.py`). Three estimators over one greedy assignment: `bound_miles` (Stage 6 optimistic upper bound), `claimed_estimate` (Stage 7 honest claim), and `greedy_assignment`/`hill_climb` (the assignment itself). Rationale: feasibility and every generation archetype answer the same "route each category to one card ‚Üí how many miles" question; duplicating it invited drift.

2. **The feasibility bound is deliberately OPTIMISTIC but currency-transfer-cap-aware.** It uses exact-ratio conversion with no whole-block flooring / min-transfer gate / transfer-timing haircut (a false "infeasible" kills a goal; overstatement is later truthed by Simulation), and floors exactly ONCE at the very end. But it DOES apply each link's annual `max_transfer_points` cap (Atlas 30k, Burgundy 2L) before the ratio ‚Äî ignoring those would pass structurally impossible goals. A dedicated golden (`test_golden_bound_floors_never_rounds_the_conversion`, travel 40k/dining 30,200 ‚Üí Burgundy 110,496 pts ‚Üí 88,396.8 ‚Üí floor 88,396) pins the flooring direction; a floor‚Üíround mutation now fails.

3. **Adjustment options are the inverse problems, computed and only emitted when they actually work.** extend_timeline (smallest horizon ‚â§ 36 mo that clears), add_card (best single acquirable addition ‚Äî only when `no_new_cards` had excluded acquisitions), raise_spend (smallest ‚Çπ10k/mo step on the best-rate category, honestly RE-PRICED per step via Stage 5's own `enumerate_opportunities` so cap-blending is respected ‚Äî the one sanctioned Optimization‚ÜíValuation call), downgrade_cabin (highest lower cabin whose locked-chart requirement the current bound clears). A saturated transfer cap correctly yields NO extend/raise option (`test_golden_atlas_transfer_cap_bounds_the_goal`): extending time can't fix a cap ceiling. `tight` = feasible but best_case < required + buffer.

4. **Archetypes are different STARTING BASINS of one honest hill-climber**, not four separate algorithms. `hill_climb` accepts the best single-category reroute until none strictly improves the cap/milestone-aware bound. This one loop IS both the blueprint's "cap-reroute" (Stage 7 failure scenario) and its "milestone-chasing greedy post-pass" ‚Äî a reroute past a saturated 2L cap or into a ‚Çπ4L DCB quarterly bonus is accepted exactly when it raises honestly-countable miles. Two adversarial goldens prove it beats na√Øve rate-greedy: milestone-diversion (dining diverted to DCB to catch the quarterly bonus twice, claim 119,834) and cap-saturation (dining rerouted off a saturated Burgundy to Infinia, claim 186,993, transfer plan sends exactly 200,000 capped points).

5. **`claimed_estimate` MIRRORS the Phase 3 projector** (same transfer cutoff month = horizon‚àí1‚àíceil(processing_days_max/30), whole-block flooring via `transfer_math.whole_block_transfer`, same milestone period semantics) so reconciliation gaps stay ~0. Verified: gap is **exactly 0** on every archetype across the integration fixtures (wallet-only, one-new-card, cap-saturation, milestone-diversion). One documented rounding-path difference (earning multiplies by Stage 5's 4dp-quantized `effective_rate` vs the projector's raw cap-split) can differ by a fraction of a point per capped card-month ‚Äî single-digit miles, far under the 10% flag, never observed on seed rates. Docstring notes it is not bit-parity; the projector wins.

6. **BR-02 (meaningful improvement) enforced at ‚â• 5% over the no-acquisition baseline claim**, applied BOTH at generation and again at exit validation (so a hill-climb drift into an acquisition can't sneak past). BR-03/BR-04 constraints (`no_new_cards`, `max_annual_fees_inr`) are hard filters before optimization AND re-checked at exit; the aggregate fee cap across multiple acquisitions is enforced at validation (individual-card filtering happens earlier). BR-05: partial strategies (not every profile category allocated) are DISCARDED, never patched. Deterministic `strategy_id = "<archetype>-<ordinal>"`, duplicates deduped by (allocation, acquisitions, transfer plan).

7. **Ranking scores simulated outcomes, weights in versioned config** (`config/ranking-weights-v1.yaml`, build rule 5: 35 goal / 20 efficiency / 15 cost / 10 simplicity / 10 portfolio-util / 10 risk; near-tie 2.0). Pipeline: reconcile ‚Üí prune (duplicates + dominated) ‚Üí hard rules (`misses_goal` never ranks #1) ‚Üí six named 0‚Äì100 sub-scores (every decision explainable, BR-04) ‚Üí weighted composite (2dp ROUND_HALF_UP ‚Üí `optimization_score`) ‚Üí select. Near-tie top-2 (Œîscore < threshold) presented as honest co-recommendation. Deterministic `headline_differentiator` (fastest / no new cards / lowest fees / simplest / balanced) for narration. Efficiency/cost are min-max relative to the candidate set (honest "best on the table"); the rest absolute. Full composite hand-computed in `test_golden_score_breakdown` (56.94).

8. **`reconcile_claim` (the deferred Phase 3 requirement) lives at the Stage 8‚Üí9 boundary in `ranking.py`:** simulation wins on disagreement ‚Äî the ranked strategy is re-labeled with simulated miles and the correction recorded in `assumptions` for narration; a >10% claim/simulation gap logs a generator-bug warning. Runs inside `rank()` before scoring so ranking never sees generator optimism.

9. **`acquirable` is a first-class `Card` field** (default TRUE; the closed state is the exception). Plumbed through domain (`Card`, `CardAggregates`), seed YAML (`axis-atlas: acquirable: false`), the DB read/write paths (`store.py`, `catalog_admin.py`), and migration `0002_card_acquirable.py` (additive, NOT NULL default TRUE, comment-documented). Atlas is filtered from acquisition in THREE independent places (`allocation.allowed_card_ids`, `feasibility._add_card_option`, `strategies._build_validated`) so no hill-climb drift or archetype can bypass it; an in-wallet Atlas is still usable (existing holders keep it). Verified end-to-end (`test_atlas_never_acquired_across_chain`).
